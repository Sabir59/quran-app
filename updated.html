Sudan is facing a colossal crisis this Ramadan.
The last ten nights of Ramadan have arrived.

Within these nights lies Laylatul Qadr, when a single act of charity can carry the reward of a lifetime. This is the moment many believers choose to give their Zakat and maximise the impact of their worship.

Millions in Sudan are struggling for life’s most basic necessities. Food, clean water and shelter have become luxuries both inside the country and at its borders.

$135 AUD will help provide food packs to 2 vulnerable families in Sudan.

Sudan’s conflict has displaced millions of families, leaving them without reliable access to food, water or medical care. What was once a hidden crisis is now impossible to ignore. Families are fleeing violence, children are going hungry, and entire communities are living in uncertainty.

The humanitarian need remains vast. Life’s essentials are no longer guaranteed.

For those fulfilling their Zakat in these final nights, Sudan is one of the places where your obligation can bring immediate relief. Your Zakat can provide water, food and essential aid to families who have lost almost everything.

This Ramadan, we are asking you to provide from what Allah SWT has blessed you with. When you give, you show mercy. You show that you see the Sudanese ummah as your own. The children as your children. The mothers as your mothers. The elderly as your family.

The Prophet ﷺ said:
"You see the believers as regards their being merciful among themselves and showing love among themselves and being kind, resembling one body, so that, if any part of the body is not well then the whole body shares the sleeplessness (insomnia) and fever with it."
[Bukhari]
Unite as an ummah for Sudan.

We cannot leave the people of Sudan to experience Ramadan alone.

MATW is delivering urgent relief inside the war-torn nation, including food packs and clean water, while also supporting displaced Sudanese families who have sought safety in Egypt and Chad. Through temporary shelter and essential aid, we are helping families survive and begin rebuilding their lives.

Providing is a direct command from Allah SWT, Ar-Razaq, the All-Provider / Sustainer:
“And spend [in the way of Allah] from what We have provided you.”
[63:10]

Every act of provision strengthens your iman. Every meal delivered, every litre of water provided, is an answer to someone’s duʿāʾ and a light for you in the hereafter.

Allah says in the Qur'an:
“Whoever saves one life, it is as though he has saved all of mankind.”
(Qur'an 5:32)

This Ramadan, continue to provide lifesaving humanitarian relief to the suffering and oppressed people of Sudan. Give in a way that allows you to answer, when asked what you did, that you did not turn away.
